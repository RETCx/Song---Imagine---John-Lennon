Objective

The learning objectives focus on enhancing students language and vocabulary development by understanding key terms from the song, improving listening comprehension through analysis of the lyrics, and exploring grammar and sentence structures with a focus on modal verbs and conditionals. Additionally, students will interpret the deeper cultural meanings, such as unity and equality, and engage in creative expression by reflecting based on the song's themes.

Activity 2 Learn Vocabulary

Listen to the audio explaining each word's meaning and example, repeat them aloud, and pause to reflect if needed. This makes learning new words fun and easy!

    1. Imagine
    • Meaning: To think or picture something in your mind.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine there's no heaven."
    • Example sentence: Imagine a world where everyone lives in harmony.
    2. Possessions
    • Meaning: Things that someone owns or has control over.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine no possessions, I wonder if you can."
    • Example sentence: We often forget that happiness doesn't come from possessions.
    3. Brotherhood
    • Meaning: A feeling of unity and solidarity among people.
    • Example from the song: "A brotherhood of man."
    • Example sentence: The sense of brotherhood among the community members was inspiring.
    4. Peace
    • Meaning: A state of calm and harmony without violence or war.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine all the people living life in peace."
    • Example sentence: After years of conflict, the country finally found peace.
    5. Heaven
    • Meaning: A place of peace or happiness, often believed to be the home of God or the afterlife in many religions.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine there's no heaven."
    • Example sentence: Some people find comfort in the belief that heaven is a place of eternal peace.

Activity 3 Learn Idioms

Listen to the audio explaining each idioms's meaning and example, repeat them aloud, and pause to reflect if needed. This makes learning new idioms fun and easy!

    1. Living for today
    • Meaning: Focusing on the present moment without worrying about the future.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine all the people living for today."
    • Example sentence: Instead of worrying about tomorrow, she decided to start living for today.
    2. Living life in peace
    • Meaning: To live without conflict, in a calm and harmonious way.
    • Example from the song: "Imagine all the people living life in peace."
    • Example sentence: Their ultimate goal was to live life in peace, free from the worries of war.
    3. The world will be as one
    • Meaning: The idea that the world would unite and people would come together as a single, harmonious entity.
    • Example from the song: "And the world will be as one."
    • Example sentence: If we practice kindness, one day the world will be as one.

Activity 4 Learn Tense

Listen to the audio on Present Simple and Present Continuous tenses with example sentences. Repeat the sentences aloud to practice, and pause if needed to reflect on how the tenses work. Take your time, and have fun while learning!

Present Simple Tense: Usage: For habitual actions, general truths, or routines. Form: Subject + base form of the verb (+s/es for he/she/it).

Example:

  • She works every day.
  • They play football on Sundays.

About John Lennon

John Lennon was a British musician, singer, and songwriter, best known as a founding member of The Beatles. After The Beatles, Lennon had a successful solo career and was a prominent advocate for peace, often collaborating with his wife Yoko Ono on activism projects. He was tragically assassinated in 1980.
